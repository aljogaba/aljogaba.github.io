EPISUIS

Epidemiology-Driven Solutions for Swine Production Systems

Overview

EPISUIS is an independent initiative focused on improving swine production systems through the application of epidemiological principles. It integrates health, production, and data analysis to support informed decision-making and long-term system optimization.

The Problem

Conventional advisory approaches in swine production are often reactive, addressing clinical issues as they arise without fully understanding the underlying system dynamics. This leads to temporary solutions rather than sustained improvements in productivity.

Our Approach

EPISUIS is based on the principle that each farm represents a unique epidemiological system. Health events are analyzed as part of a dynamic interaction between pathogens, hosts, environment, and management practices.

MAEPP Framework

The MAEPP (Marco de Análisis Epidemiológico en Producción Porcina) is a structured framework designed to evaluate and manage diseases in swine populations through an integrated approach that includes:

  • Epidemiological assessment at farm or territorial level
  • Identification of risk factors
  • Molecular characterization of pathogens
  • Analytical interpretation of data
  • Evaluation of impact on production parameters

What Makes EPISUIS Different

Rather than applying standardized solutions, EPISUIS focuses on generating system-specific knowledge through integrated data analysis and longitudinal understanding of disease dynamics.

The objective is not only to address current issues, but to anticipate future risks and enable sustainable improvements in productivity.

Outcomes

  • Improved production efficiency
  • Evidence-based decision-making
  • Better control of endemic and emerging diseases
  • Optimization of available resources
