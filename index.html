    Research

    My research focuses on the molecular epidemiology of viral diseases affecting swine production systems. I am particularly interested in the genetic diversity, evolution, and transmission dynamics of Porcine Reproductive and Respiratory Syndrome Virus (PRRSV) and Porcine Circovirus type 2 (PCV2).

    My work integrates molecular virology, epidemiological analysis, field data from commercial swine farms, and epidemiological data visualization to better understand pathogen circulation and support disease control strategies. As part of this approach, I also develop web-based tools such as DiseasesMapMx, a platform for exploring genomic surveillance data on swine pathogens.

    Applied Work

    I am also the founder of EPISUIS, an independent initiative focused on epidemiology-driven solutions for swine production systems, integrating health, production, and data analysis to support decision-making in commercial farms. Go to EPISUIS.
